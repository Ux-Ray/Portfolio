What People Were Saying

And as we listened closer, we found that most complaints weren’t about missing features but about broken expectations

We reviewed over 100 public posts from real LinkedIn users. The frustration wasn’t loud, it was quiet and steady. People weren’t asking for radical new tools. They wanted the platform to meet expectations it had already set.

Things like accurate filters, meaningful alerts, and a sense that their actions—like applying—had weight. But instead, they felt misaligned with the system that was supposed to guide them.

Application tracking issues

Users face challenges in effectively tracking their submitted applications and often find that status updates within LinkedIn are inconsistent or not provided by all companies.

"Easy Apply" limitations

Easy Apply makes things faster, but it often feels too generic. Without room to personalize, it’s easy to get lost in the crowd while recruiters get overwhelmed by volume.

Ineffective job search and filters

The job search tab delivers overwhelming results, and filters don’t work accurately—making it difficult to find relevant roles efficiently.

Inaccurate job match scoring

LinkedIn’s job fit indicator often relies on listed skills or titles, leading to misleading scores that don’t reflect true suitability.

Irrelevant job recommendations

Users are shown postings that don’t align with skills, experience, or location, making the search process inefficient.

Scam and outdated job postings

Users frequently encounter fake, outdated, or misleading ads, making it difficult to fully trust the platform’s listings.
